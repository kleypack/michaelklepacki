The years are sneaky quiet. Without a sound the hair grows thinner, the skin dappled and soft. White strands multiply slowly and silently. The bags under the eyes a lost cause. The years are felt in the aches and the stiff and the creaks. They are heavy in the bones and heavy in the heart. They steal from the mind but they are clever tricky thieves. What charges to bear? Isn’t it all still there? Want is still want, pain still pain. Still awaiting the change that never seems to come; the switch to flip but there is no switch, is there? Only the years, the sneaky quiet years, slinking along, swift in the shadows.
