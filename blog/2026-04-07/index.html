07 April 2026 at 22:58 CEST

Heavy. Hollow. Hard to describe just how I’m feeling. On the one hand: the images from Artemis II. Nearly brought to tears by the crescent Earth visible far beyond the looming lunar surface. All of us (well, all but four) somewhere on that little marble and none of us remotely perceptible. So small, indeed; and yet: the reason for it all, affirmed by those brave, crazy adventurers. The awe and dazzle evident in their voices so beautifully, painfully human. The distances we will travel, the unimaginable lengths we will push ourselves unto — only to be reminded that there is no place like home; our home and those we share it with is everything to us, all we have within the vast infinite. “We will always choose Earth. We will always choose each other.”

And all of that hope and conviction burns away on re-entry as I crash back down to the terrestrial surface. “A whole civilization will die tonight, never to be brought back again.” From up there, there is beauty and wonder; but down here, on the ground: just hatred and ego, petty and crude. “We are punching them while they’re down, which is exactly how it should be.”

Sick, actually. That about sums up how I feel. As I read these words and watch the jets and bombers take off as thousands line up hand-in-hand around their imperilled power plants and bridges; as inwardly I scream in desperation for revolt while I sit here and scroll in horror and… type. All the terror and shock and disgust that’s been mounting and multiplying for — how long has it been now? — and here we are, tipping perilously on the edge of fresh new unprecedented atrocity, yet another step far too far — how could we ever come back from this one? — and from that edge we continue to do absolutely nothing. We are all we have and yet we choose apathy, destruction, desecration, happy to pillage and rape and burn our miraculous little marble until it is barren and blackened as the dead grey moon.

Update:
Just minutes after posting this, a two-week ceasefire was announced. Which, thank god; but man, I am so fucking tired.
