Am I an opportunist for leaving? Am I a coward for staying away? I’d dreamt of leaving for so long, hadn’t I? And now I wonder if I ever should’ve left; equally filled with a desperate gnawing homesickness and dogged conviction to never return. But why shouldn’t I? In fact — shouldn’t I? Shouldn’t I fight? Not by standing in a plaza half the world away with tens of other disgruntled deserters, but by — what, exactly? What of those still manning the ship; those I left behind? In these desperate, maybe dying, days, shouldn’t we pull close, closer than ever before? Shouldn’t we have done that all along?

It is unnatural, to be so far from everything you know. Oh, what of nature in this world; but still. I don’t belong here; I am unmoored; adrift. Perhaps I simply never learned to belong. Perhaps to search, to flee, is all I know.
