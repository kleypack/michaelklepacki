To be in love with the English language, with storytelling and the power of words, but barren of ideas and bereft of skill — mute, dumb, thoughtless; cursed. As I again consider schooling, full of eager anticipation and — is it? — hope, I yearn too to hide, to cower — how can I write with nothing to say?∗ Were the chambers of my mind always so dark and desolate? How can I rediscover, if ever it was there, that spark, a story, a voice?

  1. No, the irony of writing about having nothing to write about is not lost on me — but neither is the insubstantiality of this trivial rambling, these fearful piteous grumbles. ↑
