You handed me something to drink — a water or coffee, I don’t know, it’s not important; but — something less “please, stay a while” than my mind would’ve conjured. I sipped as we spoke in the too-bright kitchen with the island feeling more like a gulf but then I was following to your room where you switched on the tv (the last act of some movie or other) and we sat on the bed, my insides all nerves and excited uncertainty. And the fear burst out stupidly, shyly, “am I missing a cue? are you waiting for me to leave?” and with a laugh it was caught and tenderly set aside — “no” — and then your hand was on mine and my gut was a-tumble in your riptide. My eyes scrunched with your smile and that simple touch became a caress and our fingers danced and traced along mere millimetres of vein and tendon, still sat perfectly side by side (were the credits now rolling?), my steady hand not betraying my quivering heart and then like a door slammed shut — or a missed step — or a bomb gone off — it was over and you were gone and I awoke.
