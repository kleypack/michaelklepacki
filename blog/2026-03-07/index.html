Lately I’ve been feeling restless. Wanting to stay up all night. It’s the depression, probably. I used to stay up for hours and hours into the night as a teenager. Usually on tumblr, or working on homework I had put off. These days I’m probably fiddling with this site (at best) or doomscrolling (not best, obviously).

It’s the depression, probably. But the reason I’m turning here, to this site surely no one but me sees, maybe, is because I’m watching a space I grew up loving be sucked of it’s soul. I used to love the web. It was somehow both a safe haven and an actual wild west within my sheltered desert home. It was where I raised Neopets until my mother needed the phone. Where I chatted on AIM with my improbable long-distance girlfriend. Where I discovered Homestar Runner and its ilk, leading to in-jokes with friends and my first ill-advised email address. Where I played games that required flash like slime basketball as my best friend sat next to me and we shared the keyboard. Where I sailed the Limewire seas to build up my music collection, because music was my best escape from the darkness but I was an adolescent with no allowance.1

But they are ruining the web. You know of whom I speak. They are stripping it of all humanity, all craft, all heart. Every day it is harder to find anything not devoid of humanity, anything you can trust, any place where you are not simply a collection of data points to be monetised. Content is king but content is dead; long live slop. They are destroying the place I love and I am angry.

And yet. I am heartened by those who still believe; who still handcraft and defend their own spaces and homes online; those who astound and inspire me with their knowledge of modern CSS and Javascript; those whose sites remind me of the AIM days; who write about music, the last films they watched, what’s inspiring them, what’s making them sad; who draw and code and read and share and type and strive and tinker and fiddle; those who fight; those who blog.2 I know I am not alone in my anger and my heart swells, if briefly, and I too want to fight.

And so… a blog. If you are here, thank you. If you made it this far, hello, friend. I hope I can keep this up. Let’s fight together.
