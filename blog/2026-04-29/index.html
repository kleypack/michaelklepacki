A week from now I’ll be stateside for the first time in 16 months. Will I be relieved of the homesickness that’s been weighing on me? or about-face and long to be back here in France? or, most distressingly, find that I simply don’t feel at home eitherwhere?1 My present is a chaos of questions strewn about like toys from a sitcom closet and I am so deeply buried I cannot see but a couple days beyond; a suffocating, untenable way to live, I know. So I return in search of answers, even as the hazy spectre of detention and violence glares threateningly from across the pond.

I cast about elsewhere, too; starting therapy again. This is a crossroads I’m not sure I trust myself to navigate unaccompanied — not a small branch but a harsh parting, with the looks of a dead end but to the keenest eye; it is brush and bramble all round, but I cannot — I mustn’t — stop; onward the only way.

  1. Altogether far too possible given my general lack of love for my destination state. ↑
