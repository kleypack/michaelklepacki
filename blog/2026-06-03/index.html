Twisting through clouds on wet mountain roads: winding black through green and grey, brake lights ahead a red gaussian blur, wiper blades a’swish-swashing through pitter and patter and the “thank you, Lord” downpour. A moment come long after the nostalgia it bore, a home-away-from-homecoming from before these roads had a name, before this place was a place, just pixels on a bright screen in a dark room with music in my ears.
