MESH 2.0 Relaunch

For months, the development team at MESH was struggling against years of tech debt and an unstable foundation. So late in 2023, the decision was made to start fresh and completely rebuild it. I too had been reckoning with what had been built before my arrival, addressing issues and bringing my own vision (based upon our new brand identity) to the app bit by bit.

Visual affordance #

A unique challenge to designing MESH is all the different types of content users can find on the platform ‚Äî from posts and reels to articles and gig opportunities. Featured content is grouped by type at the top of the home page, and below that is a reverse-chronological feed of everything a user follows. I aimed to give cards for each content type a distinct but harmonious look.

To help users find specific content across MESH, Search allows them to filter their results with chips under the search bar. Query suggestions are categorised with icons or avatars.

Subtle depth is used throughout the app to indicate interactivity and create hierarchy. The top toolbar and bottom navigation bar sit above the main content, always present and available. Content that can be opened ‚Äî such as posts on the Home page ‚Äî are presented as cards; but when opened, they simply sit atop the background. Buttons and other controls are tactile, and active input fields glow.

Posts & content #

MESHERS can tap ‚äï to create a post. 2.0 features new granular privacy controls.

Select users can also now upload reels for the first time. The segmented control in the top bar allows them to easily switch between creating classic posts or reels ‚Äî without breaking existing muscle memory.

The MESH team creates articles to share helpful information with MESHERS. These are much richer than simple posts, featuring drop caps, pull quotes, and end marks. Profiles #

Because MESH is a business-focused social network, profiles have some extra features which users are prompted to take advantage of. They can select tags that correspond with their business sector, as well as sectors they‚Äôre interested in exploring. And for MESHERS with a physical shop, they can add their address to drive business.

Profiles include multiple, prominent banner images to showcase products or services, and sector tags help with networking and marketing. If users have added a shop address, it links directly to Google Maps.

To help MESHERS find others they might want to network with, sector tags are displayed on profile cards across the app.

Animation #

We take a moment to welcome new users to MESH upon creating an account, before encouraging them to complete their profile to get the best experience.

Support emails from the app are sent off with reassurance that a response will arrive soon.

In exceptional cases when MESH must go down for maintenance, users are met with a sleepy, peaceful page assuring them the app will be back up soon.
