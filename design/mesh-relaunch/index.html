MESH 2.0 Relaunch

I created a comprehensive design system to ease design and development of MESH while addressing feedback to improve the user experience.

Near the end of 2023 it was decided that MESH would be rebuilt from the ground up to remedy years of tech debt and give the platform a new, more solid foundation. When I heard the news I knew it was the perfect opportunity to create and implement a new design system — this could speed up both my work designing the app and the dev team’s work building it.

I could also further refine the visual direction from our 2022 redesign to address user feedback. Many of them are fairly new to digital interfaces, and while they loved the look, some were struggling to actually use parts of the app. This would be a great time to enhance the user experience by bringing added affordance, structural clarity, and consistency to the visual design.

A new MVP #

Redesigning the new minimum viable product (MVP) for MESH 2.0 would put the new design system to the test and let it naturally evolve and expand. Immediately post-relaunch, the new app would be lean — hyper-focused on the most crucial features — but built atop a solid design and technical foundation.

  1. The button component includes variables for multiple sizes and styles, and dialogs have different options for leading icons and button arrangements. Table views (lists) are super versatile with many subcomponents like leading elements for icons or images and trailing elements for radios, checkboxes, or disclosure indicators. Posts can have a card appearance to signify that it can be tapped, or appear directly on the surface once it’s been opened; it also has variables for user input that include images, links, or overflow text. The top toolbar has numerous configurations depending on the page type: home, sub-level, a modal flow, a task sequence, or search.

    So many different possibilities for each component that can all quickly and easily be swapped out or specified automatically. ⤶

  2. My own personal preferences aligned very well with our user needs here! ⤶
  3. Hashtags were planned and explored (they would work a little differently than usual on MESH) but ultimately they were placed on the backlog due to time constraints. ⤶
